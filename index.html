Strong opinions

Things I actually think.

On building in South Africa
Building software here means accepting constraints that most tutorials were never designed around. Load-shedding, data costs, connectivity gaps — these aren't edge cases. They're the brief. Working within them makes you a better engineer.
On trying things before you're ready
I picked up Arduino without knowing anything about electronics. Started tutoring before I felt like an expert. The curiosity comes first. The competence follows. Waiting until you're ready just means waiting.
On GPA vs capability
A number tells you how well someone handles a specific kind of pressure in a specific kind of system. It doesn't tell you whether they can actually build things. Both matter. Neither is everything.
On this website
Most portfolio sites look the same because most portfolio advice is the same. I built this one with three modes because I'm not one thing — and I was curious whether you could tell the difference between them.
